# 📜 Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to making participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Disability
- Ethnicity
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation
- Socioeconomic status

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

✅ **DO:**
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Providing and receiving help graciously
- Asking questions when you don't understand
- Respecting others' time and effort
- Giving credit where it's due
- Being patient with newcomers

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use sexualized language or imagery
- Make unwelcome sexual advances
- Use derogatory language or personal attacks
- Engage in harassment (public or private)
- Publish private information without consent
- Engage in bullying or intimidation
- Make assumptions about people's backgrounds
- Discriminate based on protected characteristics
- Spread misinformation
- Spam or spam-like behavior

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all spaces managed by the ASRO project, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories
- GitHub Issues and Pull Requests
- GitHub Discussions
- Project Discord (if applicable)
- Project email
- In-person events organized by the project
- Any other communication channels

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct, please report it to the maintainers by:

**Email:** conduct@asrorepo.com  
**GitHub**: Create a private security advisory

Please include:
- Description of the incident
- Date and time
- People involved (if comfortable sharing)
- Any evidence or context
- How it affected you

**Note:** Reports will be kept confidential. We will not disclose reporter identity without consent, except as required by law.

### Investigation

1. **Initial Response**: Maintainers will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Thorough investigation of the report
3. **Discussion**: Communication with all parties (if appropriate)
4. **Decision**: Determination of appropriate action
5. **Notification**: Notification of outcome to reporter

### Consequences

Violations of the Code of Conduct will result in:

| Severity | Action | Duration |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Minor (first offense) | Warning | - |
| Moderate (repeated) | Temporary suspension | 1-7 days |
| Severe (harassment, abuse) | Indefinite ban | Permanent |
| Illegal (threats, violence) | Report to authorities | Varies |

escalation may occur for repeated violations.

### Appeal Process

If you received a consequence and believe it was unfair, you may appeal by:

1. Sending an email to conduct@asrorepo.com
2. Providing explanation of why the decision was unfair
3. Providing any new evidence to consider

Appeals will be reviewed by a different maintainer and resolved within 14 days.

## Conflict Resolution

We encourage participants to:

1. **Address Issues Directly**: If comfortable, speak to the person involved
2. **Be Specific**: Explain what behavior was harmful and its impact
3. **Listen Actively**: Try to understand their perspective
4. **Find Common Ground**: Work toward resolution
5. **Know When to Escalate**: Involve maintainers if direct resolution fails

## Community Guidelines

### In Pull Requests

- ✅ Leave constructive feedback
- ✅ Assume good intent
- ✅ Suggest improvements gracefully
- ✅ Celebrate good work
- ❌ Don't make personal attacks
- ❌ Don't reject on preference alone
- ❌ Don't block progress without reason

### In Issues

- ✅ Search before posting duplicates
- ✅ Provide clear details
- ✅ Be patient with responses
- ✅ Try suggested solutions first
- ❌ Don't post complaints without context
- ❌ Don't demand immediate responses
- ❌ Don't spam or create duplicate issues

### In Discussions

- ✅ Be respectful of diverse opinions
- ✅ Admit when you're wrong
- ✅ Welcome differing viewpoints
- ✅ Build on others' ideas
- ❌ Don't dismiss ideas without consideration
- ❌ Don't engage in flame wars
- ❌ Don't derail conversations

## Values

We are committed to:

### **Inclusivity**
Welcoming people of all backgrounds and experiences. We celebrate diversity and ensure everyone's voice is heard.

### **Transparency**
Being open about changes, decisions, and processes. We explain the "why" behind decisions.

### **Respect**
Treating all members with dignity. We value different perspectives and experiences.

### **Accountability**
Taking responsibility for our actions and impact. We correct mistakes and learn from feedback.

### **Collaboration**
Working together to achieve common goals. We support each other and celebrate collective success.

### **Excellence**
Striving for high quality in all we do. We provide and accept constructive feedback to improve.
